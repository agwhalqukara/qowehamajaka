FAQs about Yakov Flier

Who was Yakov Flier?

He was a renowned Soviet concert pianist and teacher, celebrated for his performances and significant influence as an educator.

What is Yakov Flier known for?

Flier is known for his mastery in performing Romantic music, particularly works by composers like Liszt, Chopin, and Rachmaninov. He was also recognized for his intense and expressive playing style.

Which music conservatory did he attend?

He studied at the Moscow Conservatory under Konstantin Igumnov, among others.

Did he win any notable competitions?

Yes, he won several prestigious competitions, including the 2nd All-Union Competition of Performing Musicians and the Vienna International Piano Competition.

What were some challenges in Yakov Flier’s career?

Flier experienced a significant interruption in his concert career due to a problem with one of his fingers in 1949, which led to a decade-long hiatus from public performance.

When did he resume his concert career?

He resumed performing in 1960 after successful treatment and continued to tour internationally.

Did Yakov Flier teach?

Yes, he was a prolific teacher at the Moscow Conservatory and influenced many prominent pianists, such as Mikhail Pletnev and Bella Davidovich.

Who were some of Flier’s students?

His notable students include Viktoria Postnikova, Lev Vlassenko, and Rodion Shchedrin.

When and where did Flier pass away?

He died on December 18, 1977, in Moscow.

What were some of Flier’s most memorable performances?

His performances of Rachmaninov’s Third Piano Concerto, especially those conducted by Leonard Bernstein with the New York Philharmonic, are particularly remembered.

How did critics describe Flier’s playing style?

Critics praised his impeccable technique, intense emotion, and ability to produce a beautifully colored and nuanced sound. However, some noted that his vigorous style sometimes overshadowed the finer details of the musical text.

What awards did he receive?
